Step 2: Create Bootable USB with Rufus

Rufus is a free tool to create a bootable USB drive from your ISO file.

1

Download Rufus

Get it from the official website

rufus.ie
2

Insert USB Drive

At least 4GB. All data will be erased!

3

Launch Rufus

  • Select your USB device
  • Click SELECT and choose your Debian ISO
  • Click START
4

Choose DD Image Mode

When asked, select "Write in DD Image mode" for Debian

5

Wait for completion

It may take 5-15 minutes depending on your USB speed

Step 3: Boot from USB

Now you need to tell your computer to start from the USB drive instead of your hard disk.

1

Restart your computer

Keep the USB drive plugged in

2

Press the boot menu key

Press repeatedly during startup:

Dell, HP, Lenovo
F12
Asus, Acer
F8 or F12
MSI
F11
Apple Mac
Option
3

Select USB Drive

Choose your USB drive from the boot menu (might be labeled as "USB-HDD", "UEFI", or by brand name)

4

🎉 Enjoy Debian!

Debian should now boot. You can try it live or proceed with installation.
